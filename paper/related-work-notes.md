# Related-work positioning (from citation research — for intro/discussion)

## Neural Cellular Automata

Neural CA learn a local update rule by gradient descent through a developmental rollout to grow images (Growing NCA), synthesize textures (Self-Organising Textures), or reach a distributed consensus label over a static input (Self-classifying MNIST); follow-ups probe robustness/control (Adversarial Reprogramming) and architecture (attention/ViTCA). We inherit exactly this machinery — a per-cell perceive-MLP-residual-tanh rule trained by BPTT — but retarget it from growing patterns/classifying to growing a functional digital circuit: a rule that develops self-repairing, position- and grid-size-invariant logic (XOR gate, 1-bit and 2-bit ripple-carry adders with a produced-then-consumed internal carry). Our distinguishing anchor is orthogonal to all of these: the trained rule, quantised to integer fixed-point, executes bit-exactly as an ordinary program on a real Z80 ISA and the same integer datapath carries a matching forward-mode gradient — a rule-and-its-gradient co-resident in one real instruction set, which no NCA paper has.

- **mordvintsev2020growing** — The foundational NCA paper: a per-cell neural update rule trained by backpropagation through a developmental rollout grows and regenerates a target image from a single seed cell, a differentiable model of morphogenesis. *Differs:* We use the same rule form and BPTT-through-development training, but grow a functional, position-invariant digital circuit (a computer) rather than a fixed image, and close the loop to a real Z80 ISA with an in-substrate gradient.
- **randazzo2020selfclassifying** — An NCA that reaches distributed agreement on the class label of a hand-drawn MNIST digit using only local communication, showing local CA rules can perform a global classification/consensus computation. *Differs:* This is the nearest 'CA-that-computes' prior art but it classifies a static input by consensus; we grow composable arithmetic (adders with an internal carry) that self-repairs, is grid-size invariant, and runs as a real ISA program.
- **niklasson2021textures** — An NCA follow-up in which the local rule self-organizes into a stationary, robust texture matching a target style, extending NCA from single-object growth to continuous pattern formation. *Differs:* It grows visual patterns; we grow computation. Our target is a truth table read at output cells, not a texture statistic, and our rule is realized as executable machine code with a gradient.
- **randazzo2021adversarial** — Shows that a pretrained image-growing NCA can be hijacked by injecting a few adversarial cells that steer the whole collective to a different global outcome, probing the robustness and controllability of NCA collectives. *Differs:* They perturb a fixed pattern-growing NCA to study fragility/control; our robustness result is earned self-repair after zeroing a patch of a circuit, and our contribution is developmental computation plus the ISA/gradient bridge, not reprogramming an image model.
- **tesfaldet2022attention** — Introduces ViTCA, a class of NCA whose local update uses a spatially-localized self-attention scheme, and demonstrates it on denoising autoencoding across six benchmarks, showing NCA architectures beyond simple perception-MLP rules. *Differs:* This advances the NCA cell architecture (attention) for perceptual tasks; we deliberately keep a minimal fixed-perception MLP rule so it quantises to a real Z80 datapath, and our contribution is the developmental-computation task and hardware/gradient bridge, not a richer rule.

## Differentiable / algorithmic neural computation

These systems learn to compute or execute algorithms by gradient descent, but each differs from ours on the axes that define our contribution. Memory-augmented controllers (NTM, DNC) and the Neural GPU learn algorithms as monolithic differentiable machines with no notion of growth, physical damage, self-repair, or spatial placement, and none is realized as an executable program in a real instruction set. Differentiable Logic CA is our nearest neighbor — it too learns a local, discrete update rule — but it targets pattern generation and rule discovery rather than developmentally grown, position-invariant digital circuits that carry their own forward-mode gradient in a real Z80 datapath. Deep-thinking recurrent nets length-generalize an algorithm by extra test-time iteration, a different invariance (problem size) than our spatial growth-and-repair, and again never leave the floating-point substrate.

- **miotti2025difflogicca** — Combines Neural Cellular Automata with Differentiable Logic Gate Networks so a local CA update rule is learned end-to-end by gradient descent yet runs fully discretely at inference; demonstrated learning Conway's Game of Life exactly and generating noise/damage-resilient patterns (checkerboard, a lizard shape, multi-color). From Google's Paradigms of Intelligence team; a 2024 interactive web article preceded this arXiv write-up. *Differs:* Our nearest neighbor: also a learned local rule, also discretizable. But DiffLogic CA grows patterns / rediscovers CA rules, whereas we grow functional, self-repairing, position-invariant digital circuits (XOR gate to 2-bit ripple-carry adder with a produced-then-consumed internal carry), and we take the trained rule all the way to a real Z80 ISA whose fixed-point datapath carries a matching forward-mode gradient — a substrate bridge DiffLogic CA does not attempt.
- **kaiser2016neuralgpu** — A highly parallel, shallow convolutional-gated recurrent architecture that learns algorithmic tasks — binary addition and multiplication of long numbers — from examples and length-generalizes to inputs far longer than seen in training, reaching 100% test accuracy on superlinear-runtime tasks. *Differs:* The Neural GPU learns an algorithm as a monolithic differentiable machine and generalizes over input length; it has no developmental growth, no physical damage or self-repair, no spatial placement invariance, and never becomes an executable program in a real instruction set. Our contribution is a locally-computed rule grown through development into a repairable, movable circuit that runs as actual Z80 machine code.
- **graves2014ntm** — Couples a neural network controller to an external memory matrix through differentiable attentional read/write, making the whole system trainable end-to-end by gradient descent; infers simple algorithms (copy, sort, associative recall) from input-output examples — a differentiable analogue of a Turing machine / von Neumann architecture. *Differs:* The NTM externalizes memory to a differentiable RAM and learns central-controller algorithms; there is no spatial/developmental substrate, no self-repair, no position invariance, and no realization as a program in a concrete ISA. Ours is a spatially-distributed local rule that develops and heals a circuit and executes bit-exactly on a real Z80.
- **graves2016dnc** — The Differentiable Neural Computer: a neural controller with a dynamically allocated external memory it learns to read from and write to, able to represent and manipulate data structures (e.g. graph traversal, question answering) learned from data rather than programmed. *Differs:* The DNC scales the memory-augmented-controller paradigm to structured tasks but remains a floating-point differentiable machine with a central controller and external RAM — no developmental growth, no self-repair, no spatial placement invariance, and no execution as a real program with an in-substrate gradient. Ours is a grown, repairable, position-invariant circuit realized on a real Z80 ISA.
- **schwarzschild2021easytohard** — Shows that a recurrent (weight-tied ResNet-style) network trained on easy instances with few recurrent iterations can solve much harder, larger instances at test time simply by running more iterations — 'deep thinking' / logical extrapolation on prefix-sum and maze-solving tasks. (Bansal et al., NeurIPS 2022, later added a recall module to fix 'overthinking'.) *Differs:* This line achieves length/difficulty generalization by extra test-time recurrence — a different invariance (problem size) than ours. It has no morphogenetic growth from a seed, no physical self-repair, no spatial placement invariance of I/O, and stays in floating point; we grow and heal a placed circuit and run it as integer machine code on a real ISA.

## Classical Cellular Automata & Universality

This lineage establishes that cellular automata are a universal computational substrate and can support self-reproduction and emergent computation — but every result here is hand-designed (von Neumann's constructor, Conway's Life, Wolfram's rule taxonomy, Cook's Rule 110 proof) or hand-tuned/evolved (Langton's loops, edge-of-chaos search), with the rule and initial configuration authored by a human. Our contribution inverts this: the local update rule is not designed or evolved but *learned end-to-end by gradient descent* (BPTT through a developmental rollout) to grow a *specified* digital circuit that is self-repairing, position- and scale-invariant, and — quantised to integers — realisable as a real Z80 program. Where classical CA demonstrate that computation is *possible* in a hand-built rule, we demonstrate that a *target* computation can be *trained into* a single learned rule and grown from a seed.

- **vonneumann1966automata** — Von Neumann's foundational lectures (edited and completed posthumously by Burks) introducing cellular automata and a hand-designed 29-state universal constructor: a CA configuration that both computes universally and self-reproduces, modelled on the logic of biological replication. *Differs:* The constructor and its rule are painstakingly hand-designed for universality and self-copying; nothing is learned. We learn the local rule by gradient descent to grow a specified circuit, and target self-repair rather than exact self-reproduction of a blueprint.
- **gardner1970life** — The article that popularised Conway's Game of Life, a two-state outer-totalistic 2D CA whose extremely simple hand-chosen birth/survival rule produces gliders, oscillators, and (later shown) Turing-universal structures. *Differs:* Life's rule is a single fixed, hand-chosen totalistic transition; computation must be manually engineered as glider assemblies. Our rule is a learned per-cell MLP trained to develop a target circuit from a seed, with self-repair and position invariance as trained properties rather than constructed patterns.
- **wolfram2002nks** — A comprehensive survey arguing that simple programs — especially elementary 1D CA — generate the full range of complexity seen in nature, including the four-class behavioural taxonomy and the conjecture (later proved by Cook) that Rule 110 is universal. *Differs:* Wolfram catalogues the behaviour of exhaustively enumerated hand-specified rules and argues for principled complexity; he does not learn a rule toward a target function. We optimise a continuous rule by backpropagation to grow a chosen computation, and bridge it to a real ISA.
- **cook2004rule110** — The proof that Rule 110, the simplest possible (elementary, 1D, 3-neighbour, 2-state) cellular automaton conjectured universal, is Turing-complete, via an emulation of cyclic tag systems using its interacting particle/glider collisions. *Differs:* Cook proves computational universality of a fixed hand-specified rule by an intricate manual glider-collision construction. We instead train a local rule to realise specific finite circuits (gate, adders) that are self-repairing, position-invariant, and integer-quantisable to a real CPU — a learned, robust, hardware-realised computation rather than an existence proof of universality.
- **langton1984selfreproduction** — Introduces Langton's loops: a small 8-state CA configuration that self-reproduces without requiring universal construction, showing self-replication can arise from a simple hand-designed rule and a loop-shaped seed. *Differs:* The loop rule and initial configuration are hand-engineered to copy a stored instruction sequence. We do not hand-design the rule; we learn it by gradient descent to grow and repair a functional computer from a seed, targeting computation and robustness rather than structural self-copying.
- **langton1990edgeofchaos** — Argues, via the lambda parameter sweep over CA rule space, that the capacity for complex information storage and transmission — the substrate for computation — is maximised at a phase transition between ordered and chaotic dynamics, the 'edge of chaos'. *Differs:* Langton searches rule space by a scalar order parameter to locate regimes where computation is *possible* in principle. We directly optimise the rule by gradient descent so that a *particular* computation is realised, and demonstrate it grows, self-repairs, generalises across placement/grid size, and runs as a real program.

## Morphogenesis & basal cognition (biological motivation)

These works establish the biological phenomenon our paper takes as motivation: robust structures (a body plan, a regenerating flatworm) self-assemble and repair from local cell-level interactions with no global blueprint or external controller, and can be read as computation over anatomical goal states. Our contribution is a learned, gradient-trained computational instance of this idea: a single local CA rule, trained by backpropagation through a developmental rollout, that grows small self-repairing digital circuits — supplying the differentiable, mechanistic model these biological accounts describe qualitatively but do not derive by gradient descent. Where this literature explains and steers living pattern formation experimentally, we show a synthetic system that grows a machine that computes and, quantised to integers, executes on a real ISA.

- **turing1952chemical** — The founding reaction-diffusion theory of pattern formation: Turing shows how two diffusing, reacting chemical species (an 'activator' and 'inhibitor') can spontaneously break symmetry to produce stable spatial patterns from a near-uniform start, giving a purely local mechanism for biological form. *Differs:* Turing's morphogens are a hand-derived continuous PDE that produces static patterns; our local rule is learned by gradient descent through a developmental rollout and grows a functional, self-repairing computer (a gate/adder), not a pattern. He motivates why local interaction suffices for global form; we supply a trainable, computational instance of that principle.
- **levin2019computational** — A theoretical framework arguing that bioelectric signaling lets cell collectives pursue large-scale anatomical goals, so cognition and agency scale up from individual cells to tissues; introduces the 'cognitive light cone' and treats morphogenesis as goal-directed collective computation. *Differs:* Levin frames biological morphogenesis as scale-free collective computation conceptually; we instantiate that thesis concretely with a learned local rule that provably grows and repairs a digital circuit, and close the loop to a real instruction set. His is the biological/philosophical motivation; ours is a differentiable, measurable synthetic realization.
- **durant2017longterm** — An experimental demonstration that briefly perturbing endogenous bioelectric gradients in regenerating planaria permanently rewrites their target morphology (e.g., two-headed worms that breed true on re-cutting), showing anatomical outcome is stored in a rewritable bioelectric 'pattern memory' rather than only the genome. *Differs:* This is the canonical evidence that regeneration follows an editable stored target rather than a fixed blueprint — the biological analogue of our self-repair result. We differ by exhibiting a gradient-learned local rule whose damaged circuits regrow to the correct computation, an engineered, quantifiable version of the robust regeneration Levin's lab documents in vivo.
- **pezzulo2016topdown** — A perspective arguing that morphogenesis and regeneration are best understood and controlled at a higher level of abstraction — as cells cooperating toward large-scale anatomical goal states — rather than purely from molecular detail, drawing on control-theoretic and cognitive framings. *Differs:* Pezzulo and Levin call for higher-level, control-oriented models of collective morphogenesis but leave the mechanism to be filled in; our learned local rule is a concrete such model, trained end-to-end so that the emergent collective behavior (grow, compute, repair, re-route) is specified by a single differentiable objective rather than described post hoc.

## Physical / in-materia / reservoir computing

These works compute in a physical or analog substrate: reservoir computing (Jaeger; Maass) leaves a fixed random dynamical system untrained and learns only a linear readout; in-materia memristor crossbars (Prezioso et al.) and deep physical neural networks (Wright et al.) train real hardware, the latter via a physics-aware in-situ backpropagation. Ours differs on three axes: our substrate is an exact, deterministic digital instruction set (a real Z80 ISA) rather than a noisy analog medium, so execution is bit-exact and reproducible; the same integer fixed-point datapath both runs the learned rule as an ordinary program and carries a matching forward-mode gradient, co-resident in one substrate; and the computer is not a static trained device but is developmentally grown from a seed by a single local rule and self-repairs after damage — capabilities absent from all four.

- **wright2022deep** — Trains deep neural networks physically embodied in controllable physical systems (optical, mechanical, electronic) using a hybrid in-situ/in-silico physics-aware backpropagation that accounts for real-world noise and imperfections. *Differs:* They backpropagate through a noisy analog physical substrate to train it as a fixed inference device; ours runs on an exact digital ISA (bit-exact, reproducible), carries a forward-mode gradient in the same integer datapath as the program, and is developmentally grown and self-repairing rather than a static trained artifact.
- **jaeger2001echo** — Introduces echo state networks: a large fixed, randomly connected recurrent reservoir whose rich dynamics transform inputs, with only a linear output layer trained — a founding formulation of reservoir computing. *Differs:* Reservoir computing leaves the dynamical substrate untrained and learns only a readout; we train the local rule end-to-end by BPTT through a developmental rollout so the substrate itself grows a self-repairing, position-invariant circuit, and that rule then executes as a real integer program carrying its own gradient.
- **maass2002realtime** — Proposes the liquid state machine: a generic recurrent spiking microcircuit (the ``liquid'') whose transient perturbation dynamics are read out by simple trained units, enabling real-time computation on time-varying input without stable attractor states. *Differs:* The liquid is an untrained generic dynamical medium with computation offloaded to a readout; our substrate is a single gradient-trained local CA rule that grows and repairs a discrete circuit and, quantized, runs as a deterministic Z80 program with an in-substrate gradient.
- **prezioso2015training** — Demonstrates a transistor-free metal-oxide memristor crossbar as an integrated neuromorphic network, trained in situ to perform a simple classification task directly in the analog device physics (in-materia computing). *Differs:* An in-materia memristor crossbar realizes a trained single-layer classifier in analog device conductances; ours is a deterministic digital ISA that both executes the learned rule bit-exactly and carries its forward-mode gradient in the same integer datapath, on a developmentally grown, self-repairing circuit.

## Autodiff/BPTT & Developmental Encodings

This cluster supplies the two methodological pillars our work rests on and the evolutionary-development lineage it departs from. Werbos's BPTT is exactly the training mechanism we run through a developmental rollout, while Wengert's and Griewank & Walther's forward-mode/dual-number AD is the primitive our quantized rule carries in-substrate on the Z80 (a single-step JVP, not the BPTT training gradient). Stanley's CPPN and HyperNEAT are the nearest developmental-encoding neighbors, but they are evolved indirect encodings that map coordinates to a phenotype without local interaction; ours is a gradient-trained local rule whose cells interact through fixed perception filters to grow, compute, and self-repair.

- **werbos1990bptt** — The foundational treatment of backpropagation through time (BPTT): unrolling a recurrent/dynamic system over time steps and backpropagating the loss through every step to shared parameters. *Differs:* BPTT is the training method we adopt, not a contrast: we backpropagate through T developmental steps of a cellular-automaton rollout to a shared per-cell rule. We cite it as the algorithmic pillar and note our twist is that the unrolled system is a spatial developmental process (grow/compute/heal) rather than a temporal sequence model.
- **wengert1964ad** — The originating paper of forward-mode automatic differentiation: decompose a function into elementary steps with intermediate variables and propagate derivative values alongside primal values. *Differs:* This is the exact primitive our substrate bridge realizes: the quantized rule carries a forward-mode (dual-number) tangent through its fixed-point datapath on a real Z80 ISA. We differ by running the forward tangent in integer fixed-point machine code co-resident with the value computation; we are explicit that this in-substrate tangent is a single-cell, single-step JVP, not the full BPTT training gradient.
- **griewank2008evaluating** — The standard reference on algorithmic differentiation, covering both forward and reverse modes, their complexity, and implementation techniques for evaluating derivatives of programs. *Differs:* We cite it as the canonical grounding for the forward-mode dual-number machinery we implement in fixed-point on the Z80 and for the forward/reverse distinction that separates our in-substrate tangent (forward-mode JVP) from the reverse-mode BPTT that actually trains the rule.
- **stanley2007cppn** — Introduces CPPNs, an indirect developmental encoding that maps spatial coordinates (x, y, distance) directly to phenotype values through a composed network of functions, producing regular, symmetric patterns without any local cell-to-cell interaction. *Differs:* CPPNs are the nearest developmental-encoding relative but occupy the opposite corner: they are evolved (NEAT), and each phenotype component is computed independently from global coordinates with no local interaction. Our rule is gradient-trained (BPTT) and is a genuinely local update reading only a cell's neighbors via fixed [identity, dx, dy, laplacian] filters, so growth and self-repair emerge from local dynamics rather than a coordinate-to-phenotype map. We also read re-stamped markers, not coordinates, for position invariance.
- **stanley2009hyperneat** — HyperNEAT: uses a CPPN as an indirect encoding to generate the connectivity weights of a large-scale neural network from the geometry of node positions, exploiting spatial regularities to evolve big networks with repeating motifs and symmetries. *Differs:* HyperNEAT is evolved indirect encoding that reads geometry to paint a static connectivity pattern; it does not grow, compute over developmental time, or self-repair. Our contribution is a gradient-trained local rule executed as a temporal developmental rollout that grows self-repairing, position-invariant digital circuits, and (uniquely to us) runs as an integer program on a real ISA carrying its own forward-mode gradient.
